## Functionality
> Functionality in terms of information security is any security-related features, services, procedures, and architectures within organizational environment in which those operate [[1]](https://csrc.nist.gov/glossary/term/security_functionality).

Reflective questions within the _**Functionality**_ include:
1. Does the SaaS offering of the application have all of the features of the on-site, internally installed offering of the same product? 
2. If not, are there any critical features absent from either choice?
3. Is the ASP aware of legal discovery and retention requirements and will they comply with a litigation hold request?

## Confidentiality
> Confidentiality is psreserving authorized restrictions on information access and disclosure, including means for protecting personal privacy and proprietary information. In simple terms, it is an ability to protect data so that unauthorized parties cannot view the data [[2]](https://csrc.nist.gov/glossary/term/confidentiality).

Reflective questions within the _**Confidentiality**_ include:
1. How does the company ensure all their information are secured?
2. How can an individual access the information? Does the company implement certain level of security hierarchy to access certain parts of information?
3. How does the company ensure an individual is allowed to access the information?

## Availability
> Availability means to ensure timely and reliable access to and use of information [[3]](https://csrc.nist.gov/glossary/term/availability).

Reflective questions within the _**Availability**_ include:
1. What SLA options are available from the SaaS offering?
2. Does the contract contain penalty clauses for SLA nonconformance?
3. Will the ASP provide metrics regarding conformance of SLAs with other clients?
4. Do the terms of the SLA meet client's business needs?


## Integrity
> Integrity is considered as guarding against improper information modification or destruction, and includes ensuring information non-repudiation and authenticity [[4]](https://csrc.nist.gov/glossary/term/assurance_of_integrity). 

Reflective questions within the _**Integrity**_ include:
1. Will the SaaS offering of the product require integration with any existing, internal, College applications/systems?
2. If so, what are the patching and upgrade coordination plans?
3. What are the network and network security requirements for such integration?

## Change Management
> Change Management in information security means a methodology that helps organization adapts to new processes and technologies with ease and efficiency [[5]](https://www.iso.org/information-security/it-change-management).

Reflective questions within the _**Change Management**_ include:
1. Are patches, service level releases and other upgrades handled consistent with expectations?
2. Are changes to the SaaS offering's environment conducted in a replica test environment before they are promoted to production?
3. Who approves such promotions? Is it approved by the organization?
4. Will we as an organization be involved in any development and testing?

## Data Access
> Data Access refers to ability to retrieve, modify, copy, or move data stored in any type of systems [[6]](https://searchinform.com/articles/cybersecurity/measures/identity-management-idm/data-access/).

Reflective questions within the _**Data Access**_ include:
1. How will the SaaS offering use client organization's data? Will the company use client's information only as client's intend them to?
2. Will client's receive a copy of the SaaS offering's privacy policy? Is the privacy policy consistent with how client's expects them to utilize the information?
3. Will the SaaS offering allow client's organization to import and export data to and from the SaaS solution?
4. Will client's have full access to all of their data at all times within the SaaS offering?
5. Is client's data completely segregated from any other clients of the SaaS offering?
6. If client's organization terminates the agreement with the SaaS offering, what happens to client's data?

## Data Security and Use Control
> Data Security is the practice of protecting digital information from unauthorized access, corruption or theft throughout its lifecycle [[7]](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/data-security).

> Use Control is a security objective considering a user's ability to perform actions on information such as download, copy, or distribute sensitive information stored or used by an application [[8]](https://www.conncoll.edu/library-information-technology/policies/software-as-a-service-saas-risk-policy/).

Reflective questions within the _**Data Security and Use Control**_ include:
1. What are the SaaS offering's stated theft-prevention mechanisms?
2. Will client be notified in the event of a data breach? How will they be notified? Is the notification timely and contractually required?
3. Does the vendor conduct third party penetration and application security test results?
4. Does the ASP company offer legal commitments with regards to their security measures?
5. Does the SaaS offering's security controls meet all of client's regulatory compliance requirements?
6. Has the vendor conducted a SAS70 or other third party audit?
7. Will the vendor share the SAS70 results?
8. Has the vendor had any breaches within the last two years?
9. Does the vendor host any data outside of the country? Do the countries where the data is hosted provide sufficient legal protections to ensure the confidentiality of client's information?

## Human Resources
> Human Resource for information security outlines all of employees’ responsibilities and roles related to information assets [[9]](https://www.mangolive.com/human-resources-for-information-security). 

Reflective questions within the _**Human Resources**_ include:
1. Are all employees of the SaaS offering's company required to sign non-disclosure and confidentiality agreements?
2. Are the employee screening policies and procedures satisfactory? Do they conduct background or credit checks?
3. Are employee accounts reviewed periodically for appropriate access?
4. If the SaaS offering's company outsources any job functions, what are the non-disclosure and confidentiality agreements, and employee screening requirements of the out-sourced agencies?
5. Does the vendor outsource any job functions outside of the country? Do the countries where job functions are outsourced provide sufficient legal protections to ensure the confidentiality of client's information?

## Physical Security
> Physical Security covers measures, policies, and procedures to protect a covered entity's or business associate’s electronic information systems and related buildings and equipment from natural and environmental hazards, and unauthorized intrusion [[10]](https://csrc.nist.gov/glossary/term/physical_safeguards).

Reflective questions within the _**Physical Security**_ include:
1. Are the SaaS offering’s data center(s) access controlled sufficiently?
2. Are appropriate environmental controls in place in the SaaS offering's data center(s)?
3. Is the company-owned data center undisclosed?

## Businesss Continuity and Disaster Recovery
> Business continuity refers to an organization’s ability to maintain critical business functions, minimize disruption and resume normal operations with minimal downtime when a crisis happens [[11]](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/business-continuity)

> Disaster Recovery also covers DRP is a written plan for recovering one or more information systems at an alternate facility in response to a major hardware or software failure or destruction of facilities.[[12]](https://csrc.nist.gov/glossary/term/disaster_recovery_plan).

Reflective questions within the _**Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery**_ include:
1. How frequently is the SaaS offering's system/application backed up?
2. How frequently is clients' data backed up by the SaaS offering?
3. Are those backups performed or transported off-site?
4. Will client's organization have the ability to perform client's own backups of client's data from the SaaS offering?
5. How quickly can the SaaS offering recover from a catastrophic failure?
6. How often are the business continuity and disaster recovery plans tested and reviewed?
